Creator:
Long Quan Tran, student assistant @ Utrecht University

Use:
https://opensource.org/license/mit/

Goals:
* To be a viable alternative to the course's legacy template by:
	- being more beginner friendly
	- using what you have learned in the game programming course
	- expanding your knowledge (events, method extensions, parent-child hierarchy
	  (trees), style, project design, state machine, and more...)
* To give students a solid foundation to work with
* To give students an idea of how they could organize their own projects.
* For you to evolve as a programmer ().

Inspiration:
Godot Engine

Feeling lost? Navigate the solution in this order:
(1) Program.cs
(2) YourGame.cs
(3) GameTree.cs
(4) States folder (contains demos)
(5) YourEngine project files (highlight GameObject.cs, Sprite.cs, and Timer.cs).

Note:
Dear students,

Just so you know, you are not forced to use
this template for your project by any means.
This template is mainly meant as a starting
point (!) for those who have trouble starting
from scratch if they do not like the legacy
template. Of course, I will be happy if you
like my template, because I put a lot of
thought into it. However you decide to shape
this template is up to the requirements of
your team's game concept.

Happy learning and game making! ;-)

Assets:
You can find many free and good looking assets online. Example:
https://itch.io/game-assets/free/tag-32x32/tag-pixel-art
It is up to you whether you want to spend (more) time crafting
your own sprites. In that case. if you are not experienced in
drawing, I recommend you to stick to low resolution (e.g. 16x16, 32x32)
sprites and restrict yourself to a color palette. Perhaps you
can find inspiration on: https://lospec.com/palette-list.
Besides that, I recommend importing textures with a default
orientation (e.g. facing right).

Final:
I love programming and video games and hopefully you will grow
to love and appreciate both more during the course. Do not
forget to have fun. Do not hyperfixate on making a professional
project and learn how to work together as a team instead. The only
way to learn how to program is by doing it and making oopsies :-)!
Enjoy!
